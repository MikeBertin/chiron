Temperature  vs time

Space play/pause · R reset. 2D Lennard-Jones melts around T ≈ 0.4–0.7. Turn the thermostat off to conserve energy and watch the crystal and liquid coexist.

Things to notice

Supercooling

Quench the liquid fast and it can stay liquid below its freezing point — then snap into a solid all at once.

Why it lags

Hexagonal packing

Left to crystallise, atoms settle into a triangular lattice — each ringed by six neighbours, the densest 2D packing.

…and its defects

Solid vs liquid

In the crystal atoms only vibrate in place; melt it and they wander past one another. That wandering is what a liquid is.
