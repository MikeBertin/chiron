Chiron

Computational Physics, in the Browser

Three classic simulations from my undergraduate computational-physics coursework — originally written in C with OpenGL around 2011 — rebuilt as interactive, self-contained web apps. Each is a small window onto how simple local rules give rise to emergent structure: fractals, magnetism, and the melting of matter.

Fractal growth

Diffusion-Limited Aggregation

Random walkers stick together into a branching fractal. Measure its fractal dimension (~1.71) live.
